Why It Matters

Seagrass meadows are one of the most diverse and valuable ecosystems on Earth, yet 29% of seagrass beds have been lost globally to date. Here in NSW, Posidonia australis, an endemic seagrass species, has faced substantial declines due to both local and global stressors, leading to its classification as endangered in several NSW estuaries.

Restoration of Posidonia in urbanized sections of eastern Australia has been hindered by the lack of suitable ‘donor’ material for the restoration, as Posidonia has declined severely in all urbanized estuaries near Sydney.
